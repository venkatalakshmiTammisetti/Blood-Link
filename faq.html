FAQs

Who can donate blood?
Anyone between 18-65 years of age, weighing over 50kg, and in good health can donate blood. Some conditions may temporarily or permanently defer donation.
Is blood donation safe?
Absolutely. Single-use, sterile needles are used for every donor, and the process is supervised by trained professionals.
How often can someone donate blood?
Whole blood can be donated every 3 months. Platelet or plasma donation intervals may vary depending on the method used.
How can a patient request blood?
Patients can request blood through registered hospitals/clinics. The request is made using our online form and includes patient details and blood requirements.
How do hospitals verify a blood request?
Hospitals must upload supporting medical documents. Each request is manually verified before being listed to ensure authenticity and transparency.
How do I know the blood I receive is safe?
All blood donations are tested for HIV, Hepatitis, and other diseases before transfusion. Only screened and approved blood is issued to patients.
What should I do before and after donating?
Stay hydrated, eat well before donating, and avoid alcohol. After donating, rest briefly, drink water/juice, and avoid intense activity for a few hours.

How quickly can a patient receive blood in emergencies?
Emergency requests are instantly notified to nearby donors. Depending on donor response and availability, patients can receive blood within a few hours.
How is blood stored and transported safely?
Blood is stored in certified blood banks at controlled temperatures and transported using cold-chain logistics to ensure its viability and safety.
